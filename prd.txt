1. Executive Summary 
Stronger Built Group LLC is a newly registered California foreign LLC (Wyoming-formed, CA-qualified 
April 1, 2026) positioning itself for state, county, and municipal government contracting. The founder, 
Roy Krautstrunk, holds a California Contractors State License Board (CSLB) Class B General Building 
Contractor license and is pursuing DVBE (Disabled Veteran Business Enterprise) and SB (Small Business) 
certifications through the California Department of General Services. 
The current website at strongerbuilt.us presents a generic modular-construction marketing page. It does 
not serve the company's actual go-to-market strategy: winning government bids across six service lines 
(construction consulting, project management, subcontracting, facility maintenance, janitorial, 
construction material supply), which will eventually expand to 10–15 lines as certifications and contract 
references accumulate. 
This PRD defines a complete website redesign that will (a) serve as a credibility asset for government 
procurement officers evaluating bids, (b) function as an always-available capabilities statement for 
buyers and prime contractors seeking subs, (c) attract commercial clients in parallel, and (d) scale with 
the business as new service lines are added. The site will be built on Next.js 14+ with the App Router and 
deployed on Vercel, optimized for speed, accessibility (WCAG 2.1 AA / Section 508 compliance), and SEO 
against government procurement keywords. 
Target launch: a Phase 1 MVP within 4 weeks, a full Phase 2 within 10 weeks. 
2. Problem Statement — What's Wrong With the Current Site 
Based on review of the current live site (homepage + services page), the following gaps materially hurt 
Stronger Built Group's ability to win government contracts and establish credibility: 
2.1 Positioning Mismatch 
• Homepage tagline "Built on Experience. Driven by Results." says nothing specific. The hero 
image (crane lifting modular structure) signals only modular construction, but the company 
offers six distinct services. 
• No mention of government contracting focus, veteran ownership, DVBE/SB certifications, CSLB 
license number, or California business registration status — the exact signals procurement 
evaluators look for within the first 10 seconds of a site visit. 
• The legal entity name ("Stronger Built Group LLC" in California) is nowhere on the site. 
Procurement officers verifying bidder identity against California SOS records will not find a 
match. 
2.2 Missing Critical Pages 
• No "Government Contracting" or "Capabilities" page. For a firm pursuing public-sector work, this 
is the most-visited page after Home. 
• No downloadable Capabilities Statement. This is the standard one-page PDF that government 
buyers and prime contractors request before considering a vendor. Not having one is 
disqualifying in many procurement workflows. 
• No Past Performance / Projects section. Even for a new firm, a transparent "we are a newly 
formed entity with these specific pending certifications and this licensed leadership" page beats 
silence. 
• No Subcontractor / Vendor Partner inquiry path. DVBE primes frequently seek DVBE subs, and 
commercial primes need DVBE subs to meet participation goals — the site should make it trivial 
for them to find and contact you. 
2.3 Thin and Generic Service Descriptions 
• Each service card is 1–2 sentences of generic copy. A procurement evaluator cannot determine 
whether to invite Stronger Built Group to bid without detailed scope descriptions, equipment 
capabilities, geographic coverage, and licensure. 
• No distinction between services delivered in-house (e.g., work covered by the B license) vs. 
services delivered via subcontractor networks (e.g., specialty trades). This matters for CUF 
(commercially useful function) compliance under DVBE rules. 
2.4 Zero Trust Signals 
• No CSLB license number displayed (California law requires license # on marketing for licensed 
contractors). 
• No insurance or bonding information. 
• No physical address, no real phone number visible in screenshots. 
• No certifications badges (coming: DVBE, SB; possibly: SDVOSB federal, DBE, OSHA 10/30). 
2.5 Technical & SEO Limitations 
• No indication of mobile-first responsive design in captures. 
• Unknown accessibility compliance — a risk for any government-facing site. 
• No structured data (Schema.org LocalBusiness, Organization), making it invisible to local 
government search intent. 
• No blog or insights section to capture organic SEO traffic on "DVBE contractor", "California small 
business construction", "government janitorial San Diego", etc.

4. Target Audiences 
The site serves four distinct user types. Every page and navigation decision should map back to one of 
these personas. 
4.1 Government Procurement Officer / Contract Analyst (Primary) 
Example: Cameron MacGregor at CHP; Angel Cervantes Jr. at Caltrans. They visit the site to (a) verify 
your firm is real and qualified, (b) confirm certifications, (c) download your capabilities statement, (d) 
check past performance. They will spend 60–180 seconds total. They need trust signals above the fold 
and a fast path to verifiable facts. 
Design implication: Home hero must contain CSLB #, entity name, certification badges, and a clear 
"Government Contracting" CTA. Capabilities Statement must be one click from anywhere on the site. 
4.2 Prime Contractor Seeking a DVBE Sub (Primary) 
Example: a mid-size GC who just won a state project and needs a DVBE subcontractor for 5-10% 
participation to meet their contract obligations. They land on your site via CalProcure supplier directory, 
or Google for "DVBE subcontractor San Diego general building." They need to know, fast: your trades, 
your geography, your certifications, your CSLB class, and how to request a quote. 
Design implication: A dedicated "Become a Partner" or "For Prime Contractors" page; service-by-service 
CSLB coverage clearly labeled; self-serve contact form with project-details fields. 
4.3 Commercial Client (Secondary) 
Example: a property manager with a 50,000-sqft mixed-use building needing ongoing janitorial + facility 
maintenance + occasional tenant improvements. They arrive via organic search or referral. They need to 
understand your range, your service areas, your pricing approach (project-based vs. retainer), and how 
to request a walk-through. 
Design implication: Services pages should speak to commercial buyers as well as government evaluators 
— avoid jargon-heavy gov-only language in service descriptions. 
4.4 Potential Subcontractor / Vendor Partner (Secondary) 
Example: a C-20 HVAC sub, a C-10 electrician, a C-33 painter, or a cleaning supplies distributor (DVBE) 
wanting to be on Stronger Built's preferred-vendor list. They arrive via word of mouth or a capabilities-
searching prime. They need a clear application flow. 
Design implication: "Join Our Network" form with trade/certification questions. This also serves Stronger 
Built's operational need: a growing vendor database for bid responses.

Purpose: In under 10 seconds, convince a government procurement officer that Stronger Built Group LLC 
is (a) a real, licensed, California-registered firm; (b) certified or on a clear path to certification as 
DVBE/SB; (c) covers the services they're sourcing; (d) worth clicking deeper.

16. Capabilities Statement download banner: full-width strip with CTA to download the PDF. 
17. Contact / CTA block: "Ready to partner?" heading, brief copy, email address, phone, and

(LinkedIn), sitemap, legal links, "Last updated" timestamp.

Services Overview page 
A grid of 6 service cards (matches the current live page's visual pattern but with better copy). Below the

7.5 Capabilities Statement (/government/capabilities-statement) 
The Capabilities Statement is a single-page PDF that government buyers download and pass around. It is 
the single most-requested document by procurement staff. Template is in Appendix A. 
Page behavior 
• The URL displays a landing page with a thumbnail of the PDF and a large download button. 
• Clicking download serves /capabilities-statement.pdf (a versioned file) and fires an analytics 
event. 
• Download is gated optionally: free download, but an embedded form offers "Email me updates 
when our certifications change." 
• PDF is regenerated (server-side or CI) whenever certifications update.

Purpose: Show (do not just tell) what Stronger Built Group has built or is building. For a new firm, this

12. Accessibility Requirements (WCAG 2.1 AA / Section 508) 
Government-facing sites should comply with Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act and WCAG 2.1 AA. 
Failing to do so can be a deal-breaker in some federal contracts, and it's a visible signal of sloppy 
execution to any sophisticated evaluator. 
12.1 Required Standards 
• Color contrast ratio ≥ 4.5:1 for body text, ≥ 3:1 for large text. 
• All interactive elements keyboard-accessible (Tab, Shift+Tab, Enter, Space, Esc, arrow keys 
where appropriate). 
• Visible focus rings on all interactive elements (never outline: none without replacement). 
• All images have meaningful alt text, or alt="" if purely decorative. 
• Form fields have associated <label> elements (visible or sr-only). 
• Error messages are programmatically associated with fields (aria-describedby). 
• Headings follow a logical hierarchy (no skipping levels). 
• All videos (if added) have captions. 
• No reliance on color alone to convey information (errors shown with icon + text, not just red). 
• Motion can be reduced via prefers-reduced-motion CSS media query. 
12.2 Testing 
• Automated: axe DevTools in CI/CD, Lighthouse Accessibility score = 100 as build-time check. 
• Manual: keyboard-only navigation test before every deploy. 
• Screen reader: test with VoiceOver (macOS) and NVDA (Windows) before launch. 
• User testing: one round with a screen-reader user in Phase 2. 
12.3 Accessibility Statement Page 
Publish a public /accessibility page declaring WCAG 2.1 AA conformance, contact for accessibility issues 
(email), and date of last audit. This is itself a government-procurement trust signal.

The Capabilities Statement is a one-page PDF (two pages maximum). It follows a near-universal format 
expected by government procurement staff. Structure:

• Company logo (left). 
• Company name (Stronger Built Group LLC).

• Contact block: address, phone, email, website.

• General Building Construction (CSLB Class B) 
• Tenant Improvements & ADA Modifications 
• Facility Maintenance & Repair 
• Janitorial & Custodial Services 
• Construction Consulting & Project Management 
• Subcontractor Network Management 
Middle Column 
Past Performance — 3–5 short project blurbs. Client name (if public) or anonymized industry (if 
confidential), project type, value range, date, scope in one line. 
Right Column 
Differentiators & Why Us — 3–5 bullets explaining competitive advantages (DVBE, CA-based, licensed, 
veteran-owned, 24/7 emergency response, etc.). 
Bottom Strip 
• Certifications: DVBE, SB, SDVOSB (federal), DBE — all with certification numbers or "In Process". 
• Codes: NAICS (3–6 codes), UNSPSC (5–10 codes). 
• Company IDs: CSLB License #, CA SOS #, EIN, DUNS/UEI (federal), Cal eProcure Supplier ID, DIR #. 
• Insurance summary: GL limits, Auto, Workers' Comp, Fidelity Bond. 
File Properties 
• File name: StrongerBuilt_Capabilities_Statement_[YYYYMMDD].pdf 
• File size: < 500KB (fits email attachments). 
• PDF version: 1.7, accessible tagged PDF (readable by screen readers). 
• Metadata: Title, Author (Stronger Built Group LLC), Subject, Keywords all set.
